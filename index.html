Young Coconut "Buko"

A young coconut, known as buko in the Philippines, is harvested while still green and immature. It contains a refreshing, slightly sweet coconut water and soft, jelly-like meat thatâ€™s easy to scoop with a spoon. Buko is often enjoyed as a drink, used in fruit salads, desserts, or blended into smoothies. The outer husk is smooth and green (or sometimes white when trimmed), and the shell inside is still relatively soft. Its high water content and tender meat make it prized for hydration and light, tropical flavor.

Matured Coconut "Niyog"

A mature coconut, or niyog, is fully ripened and has a hard, brown shell with coarse fibers. Inside, the coconut water is reduced, and the white meat is thick, firm, and rich in oil. This meat is grated or pressed to extract coconut milk or oil and is commonly used in cooking, baking, and traditional dishes. The husk is rough and dry, making it suitable for other uses like fuel or craft material. Niyog is valued more for its culinary and practical applications than for drinking.
